# Withdraw

## Overview

I applied for an internship at IBM Consulting, and initially, it was an exciting experience. I successfully passed the ATS screening and was invited to complete a technical skills assessment.

However, during the process, I realized that I lacked fundamental knowledge in Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA). Recognizing this gap, I made the difficult decision to withdraw my application.

## Reflection

Withdrawing from the opportunity was disappointing, and I consider it a missed chance. Still, it gave me a clear understanding of where I stand and what I need to improve.

Instead of ignoring the setback, I am choosing to use it as motivation.

## My Plan

I will document my journey from being a beginner in Python to reaching a strong, professional level in software engineering. This will include:

- Learning and mastering Python fundamentals  
- Building a solid foundation in Data Structures and Algorithms  
- Practicing problem-solving consistently  
- Working on real-world projects  
- Tracking progress and lessons learned  

## Goal

I may not always achieve a perfect solution or pass every test case, but my goal is to:

- Clearly explain my thought process  
- Justify my approach to solving problems  
- Understand and apply appropriate algorithms  
- Build confidence in coding and technical discussions  

## Mindset

Failure is part of the process. What matters is the ability to learn, adapt, and improve.

I am committed to becoming a better engineer one step at a time.
